The first question every consultant asks when they meet us is "what do you do?"

The honest answer is six things: web, events, software, lighting, social, and a couple of products that aren't ready to be named. The polite answer is whichever of those the consultant is best placed to judge.

We chose six practices on purpose. Not because we couldn't pick a lane. Because the problems clients bring don't respect the lines that agencies draw between disciplines. A nightlife operator needs a website *and* a venue management tool *and* a social presence *and* lighting that doesn't fall over on a Friday. Outsourcing five of those to vendors you've never met is how the work gets bad.

So we work on all six, with the same three people, in a room above a loch. The distance from the noise is a feature, not a brand story.

Most promoters work front-to-back. Decide the lineup, find a room that fits the capacity and the rider, sell tickets, hope the venue does its job.

We work the other way around. We pick the venue first: an old hall, a distillery, a glasshouse, a hill-top croft. Then we work out which artists deserve to play in a room that good. The location is the brief.

This makes booking harder. It also means every event is a place you remember as much as a night you remember. Two of the best nights we've ever done were not in any city. One was in a building most people in the town had walked past for thirty years and never been inside. The other was on a hill.

Dates only go out when tickets are ready. [Get on the list](/newsletter) if you want to know first.
